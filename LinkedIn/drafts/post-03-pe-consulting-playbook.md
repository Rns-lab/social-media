## LinkedIn

A PE firm reduced investment due diligence from 3 days to under 10 minutes.

No new headcount. No new data providers.
A multi-agent system — built in one week.

Here's the exact architecture:

Agent 1 — The Researcher
Pulls the target company's data from connected sources, scans internal documents for relevant filings, scrapes public sources for news and competitor intel.
Output: raw intelligence package.

Agent 2 — The Analyst
Scores the deal against the firm's investment criteria. Flags risk factors. Calculates preliminary valuation multiples.
Output: structured analysis brief.

Agent 3 — The Critic
Cross-checks the Analyst's output for logical gaps. Surfaces data inconsistencies. Verifies alignment with the firm's investment mandate.
Output: final pre-meeting brief, partner-ready.

Total time: under 10 minutes.
Previous time: 2–3 days of analyst work per deal.

The math:
• Average research analyst: $120K–$150K/year
• 60% of their time: repeatable research and document review
• One multi-agent system covers that entire layer
• Analyst shifts entirely to high-judgment work

For boutique consulting firms, the same architecture drives client deliverables:
The Planner decomposes the problem.
The Researcher fills the data.
The Critic reviews before it reaches the partner.

First movers aren't just faster — they're restructuring their margin model entirely.
